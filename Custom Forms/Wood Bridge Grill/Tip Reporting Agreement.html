If you receive $20 or more in tips in one month, you are required to report/claim your tips so that federal income tax, social security and Medi-care taxes, and state income tax can be withheld. The law says that you must report 100% of the tips you take home, which may include cash tips, charge card tips and any tips received through tip-splitting or tip pools.


You should keep a daily log of all your tip income. Included in this log should be all of the cash tips and all of the charge tips you received. It should also include how much you have ”tipped out” to other employees and the names of those employees. This record will help you prove you reported all your tips should your income tax return ever be questioned. The IRS provides Publication 1244, “Employees Daily Record of


Tips and Report to Employer,” that you can use to log your tips daily. You can find this form online at www.irs.gov, or you can get it from your manager.


Reporting 100% of your tips is not only the law, it is to your benefit as well:

  • Increased workers’ compensation benefits should you be injured on the job
  • Increased unemployment compensation benefits
  • Increased social security and Medicare benefits (the more you pay, the greater your benefits)
  • The increased income may improve financing approval when applying for mortgage, car, or other loans
